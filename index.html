It is widely known that, along the years, the role played by women was strongly dependant on a male-dominant society. As a consequence, women were discouraged to develop certain skills or to take part in certain events. One of such prohibited activities was the practice of sports, and that is precisely what we are going to discuss along these lines. In particular, we are going to illustrate the female participation in the Olympic Games and, even more specifically, in the athletics event. If it is true that the Olympic Games has an ancient origin, women were not allowed to participate until the year 1928. As you may guess, they did not take part in all disciplines the same year since not all of them equally "welcomed" female presence. However, do not panic within this old sexist setting since, fortunately, the situation is completely different nowadays. Currently, women can feel free to decide whether they want to practice sport and to choose the one that best fits them. Athletics events have their roots in the ancient Greek Olympic games, which were festivals composed only of athletic competitions. These ancient Olympics were strictly male affairs, as to both participants and spectators. However, Greek women formed their own Heraea Games, which, like the Olympics, were held every four years.

The development of the modern sport, however, started in the early 19th century, but not until the revival of the Olympic Games in 1896 did the sport become truly international. Even though Athletics has been part of the Olympics since its birth, it was not until 1928 that women could take part in the competition. After all the analysis done so far, it cannot be denied the fact that women play an important role in worldwide sport and, in particular, in athletics. If it is true that, globally speaking, male participation is still higher than the female one, such different is very small and, more than this, it is decreasing with each passing year. The prove is the fact that, in Europe, there are more female than male athletes. Despite this good tendency, it cannot be denied the fact that, here in Spain, athletics still has more male representatives since women only account for a third of the total participation. However, this fact does not have to discourage anybody. Undoubtedly, more has to be done in order to change these numbers of participation and, the only way of doing so, is by getting girls more involved in athletics. Our country needs brave girls willing to work hard so as to place Spanish female athletics in the world map.
